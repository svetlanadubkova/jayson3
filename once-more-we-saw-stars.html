Once More We Saw Stars

A Memoir

A devastating and beautiful memoir about the sudden loss of Jayson Greene's two-year-old daughter, and the community of friends and strangers that rallied around his family in their time of unimaginable grief.

Praise

"An utterly heartbreaking, achingly beautiful memoir about losing a child and finding hope in the midst of unimaginable grief. Greene writes with the precision of a poet and the courage of a father who refuses to let his daughter's brief life go unwitnessed."

— New York Times Book Review

"A devastating and ultimately redemptive memoir that transforms personal tragedy into universal art. Greene's prose is luminous, his insights profound, and his love for his daughter radiates from every page."

— Washington Post

"Greene has written something remarkable: a memoir of grief that avoids both sentimentality and despair, finding instead a path through the darkness that honors both his daughter's memory and the human capacity for healing."

— Boston Globe

"A masterpiece of memoir writing. Greene's ability to transform the most devastating experience imaginable into something beautiful and meaningful is nothing short of miraculous."

— People Magazine

"With extraordinary grace and wisdom, Greene has crafted a memoir that speaks to the deepest questions of love, loss, and what it means to be human. Essential reading."

— BookPage

"I loved this book. The characters feel hopelessly real in this meditation on mental illness, suicide, grief, guilt, the afterlife and AI. All these big thoughtful ideas and it still reads like a murder mystery."

— Anthony Jeselnik

"A profound meditation on love, loss, and resilience that will stay with readers long after the final page."

— O, The Oprah Magazine

"Deeply moving and beautifully written. Greene finds light in the darkest of circumstances."

— Entertainment Weekly

"A heartbreaking yet ultimately uplifting testament to the power of love and community in the face of unthinkable loss."

— Rolling Stone

"Greene's memoir is both a love letter to his daughter and a guide for anyone navigating the landscape of grief."
